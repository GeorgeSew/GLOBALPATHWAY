Germany, officially the Federal Republic of Germany, is a country in Central Europe. It lies between the Baltic Sea and the North Sea to the north and the Alps to the south. Its sixteen constituent states have a total population of over 84 million in an area of 357,596 km2 (138,069 sq mi), making it the most populous member state of the European Union. It borders Denmark to the north, Poland and the Czech Republic to the east, Austria and Switzerland to the south, and France, Luxembourg, Belgium, and the Netherlands to the west. The nation's capital and most populous city is Berlin and its main financial centre is Frankfurt; the largest urban area is the Ruhr.



Settlement in the territory of modern Germany began in the Lower Paleolithic, with various tribes inhabiting it from the Neolithic onward, chiefly the Celts. Various Germanic tribes have inhabited the northern parts of modern Germany since classical antiquity. A region named Germania was documented before AD 100. In 962, the Kingdom of Germany formed the bulk of the Holy Roman Empire. During the 16th century, northern German regions became the centre of the Protestant Reformation. Following the Napoleonic Wars and the dissolution of the Holy Roman Empire in 1806, the German Confederation was formed in 1815.

Planning Your Trip:


When planning your trip, consider factors like the time of year, your interests, and your budget. Germany offers a wide range of accommodations, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. You can also enjoy public transportation, including trains and buses, to get around the country.

Major cultures



Berlin:


Berlin's major cultures are diverse, reflecting a history of immigration and a vibrant multicultural environment. Key aspects include a large Turkish community, especially in Kreuzberg, significant Muslim, Jewish, and Buddhist communities, and a strong presence of people from former Yugoslavian and Eastern European countries. Additionally, Berlin is a hub for the arts, with numerous museums, galleries, and theaters.

Munich


Munich is a city with a rich and diverse cultural landscape, blending traditional Bavarian customs with a modern, cosmopolitan atmosphere. Key aspects of Munich's culture include its strong emphasis on tradition, its vibrant arts and music scene, and its unique culinary experiences.

Hamburg



Hamburg, Germany, is a city known for its diverse cultural landscape, stemming from its history as a major port and trading hub. The dominant culture is German, with a significant Turkish minority and other diverse ethnic groups represented, contributing to a cosmopolitan atmosphere. Hamburg's cultural scene includes a rich maritime heritage, a vibrant arts and music scene, and historical landmarks like the Speicherstadt and Kontorhaus district.

Cologne


Cologne, Germany, is a city with a diverse cultural landscape, shaped by its long history and influx of various cultures. Key cultural influences include Roman, German, and Turkish, with a significant Roman Catholic population alongside a large Muslim community and smaller Jewish and Protestant communities. The city also boasts a vibrant arts and entertainment scene, including festivals, music venues, and theaters.

Dresden:


Dresden's major cultural influences include Baroque, Renaissance, and Historism, with significant contributions from the modern era as well (Modernism and Postmodernism). The city is also deeply shaped by its history as a royal residence and its destruction during World War II, which led to extensive rebuilding and a strong sense of historical memory.

Nuremberg


Nuremberg is a city with a rich history and diverse cultural landscape, encompassing both its medieval past and its modern identity. Key aspects include its medieval architecture, its role in the Holy Roman Empire, its significance during the Nazi era and the Nuremberg Trials, and its vibrant contemporary culture.

Heidelberg


Heidelberg's cultural landscape is shaped by its history as a university town, its Romantic-era influence, and its modern status as a UNESCO City of Literature. The city is known for its historic university, its vibrant arts scene, and its diverse population, reflecting both traditional German culture and international influences.

Bonn


Bonn's major cultural influences stem from its history as the former capital of West Germany and its status as the birthplace of Beethoven. The city maintains a strong connection to classical music, with the Beethovenfest held annually and the presence of the Beethoven-Haus museum. Additionally, Bonn has a diverse population with significant Catholic and Protestant communities, as well as a substantial Muslim population and a notable percentage of people with no religious affiliation.

Frankfurt


As a city of literature, Frankfurt opens the door to a world in which books play the leading role. Anf ss the birthplace of Goethe, it is of course not only home to the famous Goethe-House, but also the German National Library - a centrepiece of literary culture.

Stuttgart


Stuttgart is a city with a diverse cultural landscape, influenced by both its historical Swabian roots and a significant influx of international residents. The city's culture is a blend of traditional German elements, particularly Swabian heritage, and a vibrant multicultural atmosphere shaped by its diverse population.
