Greece boasts a diverse range of cities and islands, each offering unique tourist experiences. Athens, the capital, is renowned for its ancient history and iconic landmarks like the Acropolis. Thessaloniki, known for its vibrant culture and delicious food, is a major city in the north. Beyond the mainland, popular island destinations include Santorini (famous for its sunsets and caldera views), Mykonos (known for its nightlife and beaches), Crete (with its diverse landscapes and historical sites), and Corfu (boasting Venetian architecture and lush greenery).


Major Cities and Tourist Experiences:





Athens



Athens is the capital of Greece. It was also at the heart of Ancient Greece, a powerful civilization and empire. The city is still dominated by 5th-century BC landmarks, including the Acropolis, a hilltop citadel topped with ancient buildings like the colonnaded Parthenon temple. The Acropolis Museum, along with the National Archaeological Museum, preserves sculptures, vases, jewelry and more from Ancient Greece.



Thessaloniki



Thessaloniki is a Greek port city on the Thermaic Gulf of the Aegean Sea. Evidence of Roman, Byzantine and Ottoman history remains, especially around Ano Poli, the upper town. The ruins of Roman Emperor Galeriusâ€™ 4th-century palace include the Rotunda that has been both a church and a mosque. Much of the city center was destroyed in the Great Fire of 1917. The rebuilt 20th-century city has a modern European layout.



Popular Islands:





Santorini



Santorini is one of the Cyclades islands in the Aegean Sea. It was devastated by a volcanic eruption in the 16th century BC, forever shaping its rugged landscape. The whitewashed, cubiform houses of its 2 principal towns, Fira and Oia, cling to cliffs above an underwater caldera (crater). They overlook the sea, small islands to the west and beaches made up of black, red and white lava pebbles.



Crete



Crete is the largest and most populous of the Greek islands, the 88th largest island in the world and the fifth largest island in the Mediterranean Sea, after Sicily, Sardinia, Cyprus, and Corsica. Crete is located about 100 km south of the Peloponnese, and about 300 km southwest of Anatolia.Crete is best known for its Minoan culture, and you can learn about these influences on a private tour of the Palace of Knossos.



Rhodes

The city of Rhodes has an Old Town featuring the medieval Street of the Knights and the castlelike Palace of the Grand Masters. Captured by the Ottomans and then held by the Italians, the palace is now a history museum.





Other Notable Destinations:






Nafplio



Nafplio or Nauplio is a coastal city located in the Peloponnese in Greece. It is the capital of the regional unit of Argolis and an important tourist destination. Nafplio is a well-liked destination for travellers looking for a mix of culture and natural beauty because of its romantic ambiance and ancient sites.



Delphi



Delphi is a town on Mount Parnassus in the south of mainland Greece. It's the site of the 4th-century-B.C. Temple of Apollo, once home to a legendary oracle. This extensive mountainside archaeological complex contains the remains of the sanctuaries of Apollo and Athena Pronaia, as well as an ancient stadium and theater. Delphi Archaeological Museum displays artifacts found among the ruins.

Planning your trip



To plan a trip to Greece, you'll want to consider the time of year, desired activities, and budget. Popular destinations include Athens, Santorini, and Mykonos. Island hopping is a great way to explore, but choose a realistic number of islands for your trip duration.
